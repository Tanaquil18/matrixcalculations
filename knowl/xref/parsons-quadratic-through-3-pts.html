Reading Question 1. Parsons Problem, Equation of a Quadratic Through 3 Points.
Select the correct steps to find the equation of the quadratic function that goes through the points \((-1,6)\text{,}\) \((1,2)\) and \((2,3)\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
A quadratic function has equation \(y=ax^2+bx+c\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—



---


\(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\) are all unknowns which need to be solved for.
ðŸ”—


 #distractor
---


\(x\) and \(y\) refer to \((x,y)\) points in the plane, so the unknowns to solve for are the coefficients \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—



---

Passing through the point \((-1,6)\) means the equation holds when \(x=-1\) and \(y=6\text{,}\) so we get the equation \(6=a(-1)^2+b(-1)+c \text{.}\)
ðŸ”—



---

The two remaining points gives us the equations \(2=a(1)^2+b(1)+c\) and \(3=a(2)^2+b(2)+c \text{.}\)
ðŸ”—



---



Rewriting the set of linear equations gives us



\begin{align*}
a-b+c \amp = 6\\
a+b+c \amp = 2\\
4a+2b+c \amp = 3 \text{.}
\end{align*}



ðŸ”—



---



Putting the equations into an augmented matrix and then row reducing gives



\begin{equation*}
\left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1\amp -1\amp 1\amp 6\\1\amp 1\amp 1\amp 2\\4\amp 2\amp 1\amp 3\\ \end{array} \right] \rrefarrow \left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1\amp 0\amp 0\amp 1\\0\amp 1\amp 0\amp -2\\0\amp 0\amp 1\amp 3\\ \end{array} \right]\text{.}
\end{equation*}



ðŸ”—



---

The solution is \(a=1\text{,}\) \(b=-2\) and \(c=3\text{,}\) which is the quadratic function \(y=x^2-2x+3\text{.}\)
