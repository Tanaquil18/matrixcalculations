Activity 1.1.7.

(a)
Consider the equation \(x+y=2\text{.}\) When \(x=0\) and \(y=2\text{,}\) the equation is true, so I’ve plotted the point \((0,2)\text{.}\) Think of at least \(3\) more values of \(x\) and \(y\) that satisfy the equation, and plot them as points. Typing the point in a row below in the format \((x,y)\) will plot the point.
What do you notice about the visual relationship between the points on the graph, that is, points that satisfy the equation \(x+y=2\text{?}\)
Hint.
Scroll down to the lower rows of the graph interactive, activate and animate the point found there.
(b)
Now consider the equation \(15x+6y=15\text{.}\) When \(x=1\) and \(y=0\text{,}\) the equation is true, so I’ve plotted the point \((1,0)\text{.}\) Think of at least \(3\) more values of \(x\) and \(y\) that satisfy the equation (your answers don’t have to be whole numbers!), and plot them as points by typing each one in \((x,y)\) format in a row below.
What does the graph of \(15x+6y=15\) look like?
